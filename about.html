--- layout: single title: About me permalink: / author_profile: true classes: wide --- I am Yue Gu, a recently graduated Ph.D. at MD Anderson Cancer Center and the University of Texas School of Public Health from Texas Medical Center. Over the past several years, I worked at MD Anderson Cancer Center to study the genomic mechanism of Cancer. Under the mentorship of Drs. John Paul Shen, Luis Leon-Novelo, and Traver Hart, I designed and developed a simulation framework to model genetic interactions in CRISPR screening, which formed the core basis of my dissertation research.

Background

I hold a Ph.D. in Biostatistics and Data Science with minor in Health Economics and Breadth in Bininformatics from the University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston (2025), a M.S. in Biostatistics from Columbia University in the city of New York (2020), and a B.S. double major in Mathematics and Statistics from the University of Iowa (2017). My research career starts from the University of Iowa as a research intern, where I worked on the parametric testing and statistical modeling on range of movements from burnt patients in the Department of Surgery. This experience ignited my passion for applying statistical methods to solve real-world biomedical problems. During my study at Columbia University, following the lead of Drs. Hanga Galfalvy and Melanie Wall, I developed reproducible clustering analysis emphasizing on patients in Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), Bipolar, and Remitted Depressed Study Group. These early projects laid a solid foundation for my subsequent academic research and inspired me to pursue a Ph.D. in seeking answers on how we may leverage statistical rigors to complex biomedical questions.

Research Interests

My research lies in the intersection of biomedical sciences and public health. In my doctoral studies, I span multi-disciplinary research in computational biology and statistical methodology development, centered on modeling the dynamics of complex biological systems and applying novel mathematical models to advance the combinatorial Cancer treatments. My interested areas are:
  • Cancer Genomics; Somatic Mutation Calling; Genome Analysis; Molecular Oncology Profiling
  • Monte-Carlo Methods; Simulation; Statistical Modelling; Reproducible Analysis
  • Computational and Mathematical Biology; Genetic Interactions; CRISPR Simulation Design
